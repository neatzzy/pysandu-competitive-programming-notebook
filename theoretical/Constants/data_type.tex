\subsection{Data type limits}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Integer limits}: The typical 32 bit signed integer bounds are $(\approx \pm 2 \times 10^9)$ and is obtained by using the \texttt{INT\_MAX} and \texttt{INT\_MIN} constants, while the 64 bit signed integer bounds are $(\approx \pm 9 \times 10^{18})$ and is obtained by using the \texttt{LLONG\_MAX} and \texttt{LLONG\_MIN} constants.
    \item \textbf{Floating-point limits}: The typical 32 bit floating-point bounds are approximately $\pm 3.4 \times 10^{38}$ and is obtained by using the \texttt{FLT\_MAX} and \texttt{FLT\_MIN} constants, while the 64 bit floating-point bounds are approximately $\pm 1.7 \times 10^{308}$ and is obtained by using the \texttt{DBL\_MAX} and \texttt{DBL\_MIN} constants.
\end{itemize}
