\subsection{Algorithms/Concepts}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Convex Hull (Monotone Chain or Graham Scan): }Finds the smallest convex polygon containing all points. O($n \log n$). Monotone Chain is simpler to implement.
    \item \textbf{Line Segment Intersection: }Two segments $AB$ and $CD$ intersect iff $A, B$ are on opposite sides of line $CD$ AND $C, D$ are on opposite sides of line $AB$. Check using cross products.
    \item \textbf{Point in Polygon: }Winding number or ray casting in O($n$). Count how many times a ray from the point crosses polygon edges; odd = inside.
    \item \textbf{Angle of vector: }Use \texttt{atan2(y,x)} to find the angle in $(-\pi, \pi]$. Prefer cross/dot products over angles to avoid float precision issues.
    \item \textbf{Closest Pair of Points: }Divide and conquer in O($n \log n$). Split by median $x$, recurse, then check strip of width $2d$ around the dividing line (at most 8 candidate points per side).
    \item \textbf{Rotating Calipers: }Technique to solve problems on convex hulls (diameter, width, minimum bounding rectangle) in O($n$) after the hull is built. Maintain two antipodal points and rotate them around the hull.
\end{itemize}
